1950 - "Turing test"

Alan Turing proposed the Turing test: In 1950, Turing proposed the famous Turing test, which is a standard for measuring whether a computer has intelligence. The test involves a person determining whether they are conversing with a human or a computer based on the conversation.

1956 - "Artificial Intelligence"

In 1956, John McCarthy and a group of researchers proposed the concept of "Artificial Intel ligence" at the Dartmouth Conference, and defined it as "the study and design of intelligent agents, where a n intelligent agent is a system that perceives its environment and takes actions that maximize its chan ces of success in achieving its goals".

1980 - "deep learning"

In the late 1980s and early 1990s, deep learning began to emerge as an important branch of artificial intelligence. Deep learning models can simulate human neural networks and achieve complex pattern recognition and prediction tasks through connections between multiple layers of neurons.

2011 - "IBM's Watson artificial intelligence"

In 2011, IBM's Watson artificial intelligence system wo n the famous American TV game show Jeopardy!, becoming an important mi lestone in the field of artificial intelligence.
